    IDA Publications (5-10)

    This Practice describes the types of formal IDA publications, and the policies and procedures for their review and release.

    Practice No.: No. 5-10 (Revised)

    Effective Date: February 27, 1989

    Purpose

    This Practice describes the types of formal IDA publications, and the policies and procedures for their review and release.

    Applicability

    This Practice applies to all elements of IDA except the Communications Research Division and the Supercomputing Research Division.

    Policy

    A permanent record of results from IDA's research and analysis program, whether sponsored or independent, will normally be produced as a formal IDA publication in one of the following types of documents:

    1. Report 2. Group Report 3. Paper 4. Document

    The need to produce these formal documents is in no way intended to discourage or preclude the dissemination of IDA research material through professional journals and commercial or university presses. Such dissemination is encouraged, provided that regulations with regard to classification, review, and release for publication are followed.

    The documents differ principally in the degree of review to which they are subjected, and the scope of the work involved. Thus, for example, an annotated briefing might be forwarded as an IDA Document or as an IDA Paper. Publications intended simply to convey a record of data or events would generally be published as an IDA Document.

    Definitions

    Reports

     

    Reports are intended to be the most authoritative and most carefully considered products IDA publishes. They will normally embody the results of major projects which (a) have a direct bearing on decisions affecting major programs, or (b) address issues of significant concern to the Executive Branch, the Congress and/or the public, or (c) address issues that have significant economic implications. Reports will be numbered serially with the prefix "R-".

     

    Group Reports

     

    Group Reports are also authoritative and carefully considered products recording the findings and results of IDA established working groups and panels comprised of senior individuals addressing major issues which otherwise would be the subject of an IDA Report. Group Reports will be numbered serially with the prefix "GR-".

     

    Papers

     

    Papers will normally address relatively restricted technical or policy issues. They will normally not be used to communicate the results of major projects, but rather of special analyses, interim reports or phases of a task, ad hoc or quick reaction work, and the like. Papers will be numbered serially with the prefix "P-".

     

    Documents

     

    IDA Documents will be used for the convenience of the sponsors or the analysts (a) to record substantive work done in quick reaction studies, (b) to record the proceedings of conferences and meetings, (c) to make available preliminary and tentative results of analyses, (d) to record data developed in the course of an investigation, or (e) to forward information that is essentially unanalyzed and unevaluated. Documents will be numbered serially with the prefix "D-".

    Degree of Endorsement

    IDA has a institutional responsibility for the quality of the professional work that goes into the formal written matter it issues. In every case IDA staff will take care to ensure that materials forwarded for release meet the highest professional standards, given their purposes and intended audiences. IDA Documents, used for convenience of sponsors or analysts to record results, data or proceedings of meetings that may not be completely evaluated or analyzed, must clearly state the source and nature of the information, the basis and limitations of any conclusions and the extent of any formal IDA review. IDA Papers receive a higher degree of Corporate endorsement, in that IDA warrants the thoroughness, objectivity and appropriate analytical methodology of the contents and that the results, conclusions and recommendations are properly supported by the material presented. IDA Reports receive the highest degree of Corporate endorsement, in that IDA stands behind the conclusions and recommendations contained therein. IDA Group Reports also receive the highest degree of Corporate endorsement. They differ only in that the work reported is the result of a working group or panel consisting of senior individuals that has been established by IDA to address particularly significant issues.

    Responsibilities

    Responsibility for the quality of IDA's professional work begins with the individual staff member and extends to the project leader, the program manager, the division management, and the headquarters staff. In each instance the Division Director is responsible for assuring the adequate and timely substantive review is accomplished, for obtaining the necessary security reviews, and for obtaining distribution approvals required under sponsored research projects. For every approved task order, Division Directors shall at inception apprise the Vice President, Planning and Evaluation and Vice President, Research, of the nature of the product expected, the schedule of reviews and the personnel of the review committee.

    Review

    A formal review panel will be established for all projects expected to produce an IDA Report or for any project of major size and scope similar to one which would produce a Report, but for which that format is not appropriate. The cognizant Division Director will select the review panel with the advice and approval of the Vice President, Research or his designee. The panel will include knowledgeable individuals from the IDA staff and from outside IDA who can be expected to provide a constructive critical review. While staff members from the division responsible for the report may be included on the review panel, they should not normally constitute a majority of the panel. The Vice President, Research will designate a member of each panel to be the Senior Reviewer to advise him on the progress of the project and the quality and releasability of the Report.

    The review panel will normally meet three times during the course of the project: first, on completion of the definition phase when the project scope, content and research plan are established; second, during the course of the project when substantive work has been essentially completed; and third, when a final draft of the Report is available.

    The review panel will address the questions of whether the Report meets the standards set forth in "IDA Principles of Operation" (IDA Practice No. 9-01), whether it is of sufficient relevance, substance and professional quality to warrant publication, and whether it is responsive to the task order and the problem posed by the sponsor. The panel should also affirm that the stated results, conclusions and recommendations flow from the analysis, that judgments are appropriately identified as such, and it should identify errors or omissions of fact, logic or analysis, and implicit assumptions and possible biases, as well as suggesting improvements in the organization and presentation of the material.

    The review panel will report to the Division Director. The Vice President, Research will be kept informed of the project progress and review panel activities by the Senior Reviewer on the review panel.

    For IDA Group Reports, the review process will be the same as for the IDA Reports except that the formal review panel will consist of one or more of the senior individuals of the working group or panel and at least one knowledgeable individual not directly involved in the working group or panel to act as the Senior Reviewer. The review panel will meet on the same schedule, address the same questions and report as if they were reviewing an IDA Report.

    For IDA Papers, two or more individuals within or outside IDA will be designated to review the Paper prior to its publication. Comments of the reviewers will be made in writing to the Division Director responsible for the project. They should address the same questions as set forth above for a review panel. Review should be equivalent to that of a refereed paper in a professional journal.

    Review procedures for IDA Documents will be established by the cognizant Division Director. The degree of review will be appropriate to the content and intended use of the document, and must be indicated in a preface to aid the reader.

    Release

    Upon satisfactory completion of the review process and final modification and editing of the document, it will be forwarded to the appropriate IDA official for release. For sponsored research, release transfers to the sponsor authority for publication and distribution, although IDA may publish and make distribution of documents as the sponsor requests.

    IDA Reports and Group Reports will be released by the President of IDA or, in his absence, the Vice President, Research. IDA Papers will be released by the cognizant Division Director. If the topic or substance of a Paper may, in the judgment of the Division Director, be sensitive or controversial this should be brought to the attention of the Vice President, Research or his designee, for decision on any variations in review and release procedures. IDA Documents will be released by the cognizant Division Director. For independent research products, Division Directors will be responsible for release and distribution, subject to the same caveat regarding sensitivity as in the case of IDA Papers. In all cases publication should follow the procedures of the IDA Publications Guide.

    Security Clearance And Distribution Approval

    Contract provisions applying to most of the sponsored research program require that the sponsoring agency provide security review and approve the distribution of any document produced under contract. Such review and approval should be arranged by the cognizant Division Director.

    External approval of the distribution of a publication resulting from the independent research program is not required unless the publication is classified. If there is any uncertainty about whether it is classified, or if classified material was used in preparing it, security review should be requested, with a clear indication that the document was produced under the IDA Independent Research Program.

    Disclaimers

    The inside cover of each of the formal IDA publications will contain definitions of the types of publications, an acknowledgment of the source of support and a disclaimer, as follows:

    A. Definitions.

    IDA publishes the following documents to report the results of its work.

     Reports

    Reports are the most authoritative and most carefully considered products IDA publishes. They normally embody results of major projects which (a) have a direct bearing on decisions affecting major programs, (b) address issues of significant concern to the Executive Branch, the Congress and/or the public, or (c) address issues that have significant economic implications. IDA Reports are reviewed by outside panels of experts to ensure their high quality and relevance to the problems studied and are released by the President of IDA.

    Group Reports

    Group Reports record the findings and results of IDA established working groups and panels composed of senior individuals addressing major issues which otherwise would be the subject of an IDA Report. IDA Group Reports are reviewed by the senior individuals responsible for the project and others as selected by IDA to ensure their high quality and relevance to the problems studied, and are released by the President of IDA.

    Papers

    Papers, also authoritative and carefully considered products of IDA, address studies that are narrower in scope than those covered in Reports. IDA Papers are reviewed to ensure that they meet the high standards expected of refereed papers in professional journals or formal Agency reports.

    Documents

    IDA Documents are used for the convenience of the sponsors or the analysts (a) to record substantive work done in quick reaction studies, (b) to record the proceedings of conferences and meetings, (c) to make available preliminary and tentative results of analyses, (d) to record data developed in the course of an investigation, or (e) to forward information that is essentially unanalyzed and unevaluated. The review of IDA Documents is suited to their content and intended use."

    B. For sponsored research results [except in cases where contract provisions require otherwise]:

    "The work reported in this document was conducted under Contract No. ________for___________ . The publication of the IDA [Report, Paper, etc.] does not indicate endorsement by the [client, Department or Agency] nor should the contents be construed as reflecting the official position of that Agency."

    C. For independent research work:

    "The work reported in this publication was conducted under IDA's Independent Research Program. Its publication does not imply endorsement by the Department of Defense or any other Government agency, nor should the contents be construed as reflecting the official position of any Government Agency."

    D. The following additional disclaimers shall be used for the publications indicated:

    Reports [None]

    Group Reports [None]

    Papers

    "This Paper has been reviewed by IDA to assure that it meets the high standards of thoroughness, objectivity, and appropriate analytical methodology and that the results, conclusions and recommendations are properly supported by the material presented."

    Documents

    "This Document is published in order to make available the material it contains for the use and convenience of interested parties. The material has not necessarily been completely evaluated and analyzed, nor subjected to formal IDA review."
