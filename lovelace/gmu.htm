George Mason University (GMU)

GMU is a state university in Fairfax, Virginia (USA) which grants bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees. It has approximately 20,000 students (13,000 undergraduate, 7,000 graduate). It's named after George Mason, the author of the Virginia Bill of Rights; the U.S. Bill of Rights is based on George Mason's work.
