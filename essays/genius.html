How to be a Genius

David A. Wheeler

2016-01-16

Be curious History of Rome, etc. Ramp up your reading/ listening/ watching speed Be a specialist and a generalist Learn from little projects Anticipate. Work across ages. Some areas are youth-obsessed; they (incorrectly) assume you can't learn from the past. Other areas are older-person-obsessed; they (incorrectly) assume that younger people have no wisdom to provide. Neither are right. Build systems in ways to avoid or detect mistakes. Everyone makes mistakes. Pick designs that are less mistake-prone, or at least make it easy to find the problem if you make a mistake. I'm good at multitasking, and when possible, I avoid it (easy to make a mistake, hard to debug). Use tools to detect mistakes ahead of time.

Feel free to see my home page at https://dwheeler.com. You may also want to look at my paper Why OSS/FS? Look at the Numbers! and my book on how to develop secure programs.
