Green FLOSS (Free/Libre/Open source software)

David A. Wheeler

2008-12-22

http://en.wikipedia.com/wiki/Green_computing aka Green IT You asked me about the connection between OSS & green. Here are some thoughts: * Lower power consumption. OSS can be modified/reconfigured to remove unneeded functionality and use less power. Extreme example: OLPC, where they recoded all sorts of things (often to remove unneeded wakeups), producing massively lower power use. * Longer life for computer hardware, so less environmentally-damaging waste. Computer hardware is terrible to the environment when thrown away: toxic metals (mercury, lead, etc.), non-degradable plastics, etc. It's also a waste of gold, etc. Refresh rates for hardware are often unrelated to user need, but instead driven by software makers' need to increase functionality so you'll re-buy the product. (Key example: Vista. Did anyone REALLY need Aero? But they had to buy new hardware!). So instead, keep longer, or repurpose existing hardware. * Less need for hardware, due to hardware consolidation & need for fewer resources. Result: less consumption of environmental resources in the first place. OSS tends to be much more efficient at using hardware, because anyone can fix a performance problem. Also, licensing rules don't limit virtualization or other sharing, so you can easily share. Thin clients/web clients are easily implemented with OSS. (See http://k12ltsp.org).
