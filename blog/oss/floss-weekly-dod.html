FLOSS Weekly: FLOSS in the DoD

FLOSS Weekly #160 is out, and it features me! It’s an interview of me by Randal Schwartz and Simon Phipps about free/libre/open source software (FLOSS) and the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD). The DoD uses and releases FLOSS, and it has a recent policy about FLOSS that I think is pretty good. If you’re interested in this topic, take a look!

If you’re interested in this topic, you might also be interested in:

  1. DoD information on open source software. In particular, look at the DoD OSS policy (“Clarifying Guidance Regarding Open Source Software (OSS)”, Memorandum by David M. Wennergren, 16 October 2009), the DoD OSS FAQ, and the paper “Use of Free and Open-Source Software (FOSS) in the U.S. DoD” (MITRE 2003).
  2. DACS Software Tech News issue on “DoD and Open Source Software”. See especially my essay, “Publicly Releasing Open Source Software Developed for the U.S. Government”
  3. David A. Wheeler’s personal website (yes, that’s my website). You might be especially interested in “Why Open Source Software / Free Software (OSS/FS)? Look at the Numbers!” (a huge collection of FLOSS statistics), “FLOSS is commercial software” , “Make Your Open Source Software GPL-Compatible. Or Else” (Why you should pick a GPL-compatible license), “More than a Gigabuck: Estimating GNU/Linux’s size” , “How to Evaluate OSS/FS Programs” , “Secure Programming for Linux and Unix HOWTO” (information on creating secure software), and “Fully Countering Trusting Trust through Diverse Double-Compiling (DDC)” (on countering the “Trusting Trust” attack).

So take a look at FLOSS Weekly #160, and enjoy.
